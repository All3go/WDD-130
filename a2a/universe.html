Constellations Universe

Earth

Our home planet, about 12,742 km in diameter.

Moon

Earth’s natural satellite, around 3,474 km in diameter.

Jupiter

The largest planet in our solar system, about 139,820 km in diameter.

Sun

The star at the center of our solar system, roughly 1.39 million km in diameter.

Solar System

A system of planets orbiting our Sun, spanning about 287.46 billion km across.

Milky Way Galaxy

Our galaxy, containing over 100 billion stars, is about 100,000 light-years across.

Neighborhood Galaxy Cluster

The Local Group, a cluster of more than 50 galaxies including the Milky Way and Andromeda, spans about 10 million light-years.

Super Cluster

The Virgo Supercluster is a massive collection of galaxy clusters, stretching about 110 million light-years.

Hercules–Corona Borealis Great Wall

The largest known structure in the universe, measuring about 10 billion light-years across.
